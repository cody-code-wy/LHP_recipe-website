Baked Potato Soup

Switch to Print Mode

Mise en Place

Prep for cooking

Collect Ingredients

  • 5-8 Large baking potatoes
  • 1L Beef/Chicken Stock (Vegitable works for vegitarian version)
  • 200-300 Grams aged Cheddar cheese
  • 450 Grams sour cream
  • 1-2 Cups chopped scallion
  • 1/2 Cup flour
  • 1 Tbsp butter
  • 1 Tbsp salt
  • 2 Cups milk
  • 150-200 Grams bacon bits (One bag premade will normally surfice)

Vegan

For vegan cooks, the milk, sour cream, and cheddar can be removed without any direct replacement. The butter can be replaced with an oil (such as extra virgin olive oil). Bacon bits substitutes normally work very well, but can be omitted if desired. Using a good vegan cheddar can make a much better soup.

Dialysis

When preparing this dish for any person on dialysis, dice the potatoes into 1cm cubes or smaller and soak in water (tap is fine) for 2-3 hours, and halve the salt. Do not purchase 'low sodium' stock,sour cream,or bacon bits. Low sodium products normally are higher in potassium, wich is worse than sodium for dialysis patients.

Collect tools

You will need the following tools, have them at hand

  • Plastic/wooden stirring spoon
  • Large pot
  • Small sauce pan
  • Whisk
  • Knife
  • serving ladle

Begin Prepping ingredients

  1. Warm butter to room temperature, just leave it on the counter in the packaging for about a hour, or until soft
  2. Shread all cheddar chese
  3. Wash and cube potatoes into 5-1cm cubes. Larger cubes make a more dense soup with a strong potato flavor, Smaller cubes makes a lighter more flavorfull soup
  4. Chop all scallions

Your mise en place is complete, time to start cooking

Cooking

  1. Bring 6-8 cups of water to boil
  2. Add diced potatoes
  3. Add Stock
  4. Add sour cream
  5. Add salt
  6. Add milk

Making a Roux

This step is optional, skip this step if you want a thin soup.

  1. Turn soup temp to low
  2. Put butter in sauce pan
  3. Turn on heat on very low for sauce pan
  4. Add flour; For a thicker soup add more flour
  5. Stir the souce pan constantly until the mixture turns light brown, like the picure above
  6. Add roux to soup, and stir vigrously
  7. Turn soup temp back up
  1. Add most of the cheddar cheese, keep some aside as a garnish
  2. Add most of the cheddar cheese, keep some aside as a garnish
  3. Wait for all of the cheese to melt into the soup
  4. Add half of the bacon bits
  5. Take the soup off heat

Serve

  1. Portion soup into bowls
  2. Top each bowl with some of the following, to taste
    • Cheddar chese
    • Chopped scalions
    • Bacon bits
  3. Serve and enjoy

This dish goes very well with a fresh garden salad
